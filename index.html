Hi, I'm Davide Cantavenera.

Assistant Researcher in Visual Perception & Cognitive Neuroscience.

Using behavioral experiments and non-invasive brain stimulation and neuroimaging techniques, I study the neural mechanisms underlying visual perception and how the brain constructs our perceptual experience from sensory input. Curriculum Vitae

Experience

Research Internship — tACS Challenge Project, DPG

Conducting an independent research internship within the Department of General Psychology, focused on the causal role of neural oscillations in visual perception using transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS):

  • Designing and implementing tACS entrainment protocols (alpha-band, 10 Hz) targeting primary visual cortex (V1) and motion-sensitive area MT/V5.
  • Investigating phase-dependent modulation of the Flash Drag Effect to dissociate feedforward detection from feedback correction mechanisms.
  • Programming stimulus presentation and online phase-tracking routines in PsychoPy and jsPsych.
  • Analyzing oscillatory and behavioral data within a Bayesian inferential framework using Python and R.

Research Internship — Visual Perception Lab, DPG

Carried out empirical research on visual perception within a cognitive neuroscience laboratory, contributing across the full research pipeline from study conceptualization to manuscript preparation:

  • Designed and pre-registered psychophysical experiments on visual illusions (e.g., Flash Drag Effect), including participant recruitment and sample-size justification.
  • Administered behavioral paradigms and transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS) protocols in controlled laboratory settings.
  • Developed experimental scripts in jsPsych and PsychoPy, including adaptive staircase procedures and stimulus generation pipelines.
  • Performed frequentist and Bayesian statistical analyses in Python and R, and contributed to academic reporting and manuscript drafting.

AI Evaluation Internship — R-TREE TECHNOLOGIES S.R.L.

Applied systematic evaluation methodologies to assess the output quality of Large Language Models (LLMs), bridging cognitive science principles with AI quality assurance:

  • Developed and applied structured annotation rubrics to evaluate contextual coherence, factual accuracy, and reasoning quality of LLM-generated content.
  • Conducted comparative analyses across models, synthesizing quantitative performance metrics into technical reports for the development team.
